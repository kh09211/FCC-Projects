Tribute to Paul Anderson

The strongest man who ever lived

Strongman Paul Anderson holds a bending bar as nearly 1000 pounds rest on his back



Facts about Paul's life:


  • Paul Anderson was born in Toccoa, Georgia on October 17, 1932.

  • The only man of his strength to be confirmed drug free since steroids had not been invented yet.

  • As a teenager, he began his early weight training on his own in his family's backyard to increase his size and strength so that he would be able to play on the Toccoa High School football team.

  • He used special homemade weights that his father created out of concrete and other backyard methods of strength training.

  • Paul eventually met weightlifter Bob Peoples, who would greatly influence him in squat training and introduce him into weightlifting circles.

  • In 1955, at the height of the Cold War, Anderson traveled to the Soviet Union and was winner of the USA National Amateur Athletic Union Weightlifting Championship. He recieved a standing ovation.

  • During the 1955 World Championships in Munich, Germany that October, Anderson went on to establish two other world records

  • In 1961, Anderson and his wife Glenda founded the Paul Anderson Youth Home, a home for troubled youth in Vidalia, Georgia.

  • He would perform stunts such as hammering a nail with his bare fist and raising a table loaded with eight men onto his back.

  • The Guinness Book of World Records (1985 edition) lists his feat of lifting 6,270 pounds (2,850 kg) in a back lift as "the greatest weight ever raised by a human being"

  • In 1960, Anderson married Glenda Garland. The couple were devout Christians

  • As a child, Anderson suffered from Bright's disease (now known as chronic nephritis), a kidney disorder, and he eventually died from kidney disease.
